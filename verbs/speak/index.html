SPEAK

0/9

I. say something in order to convey information or to express a feeling
"in his agitation he was unable to speak"

1. have a conversation
"last time we spoke, you told me you couldn't do the job"

2. utter (a word, message, etc.)
"patients copy words spoken by the therapist"

3. communicate in or be able to communicate in (a specified language)
"my mother spoke Russian"

4. make a speech or contribute to a debate
"twenty thousand people attended to hear him speak"

II. (of behaviour, an object, etc.) serve as evidence for something
"his frame spoke tiredness"

1. show (someone or something) to be in a particular state or to possess a certain quality
"she had seen nothing that spoke him of immoral habits"

III. (of a musical instrument or other object) make a sound when functioning
"the gun spoke again"

1. (of a hound) bark.
